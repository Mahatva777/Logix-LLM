# Infosys — Filings & Guidance Disclosures

SOURCE: Curated from Infosys's official quarterly results disclosure (infosys.com) and Economic Times live coverage of Infosys Q4 FY26 results.

DATE: 2026-04 (Q4 FY26 results reporting window)

## Dividend Declaration

As part of its Q4 FY26 results disclosure, Infosys's board declared a final dividend of ₹25 per share. This is a formal capital-return commitment disclosed alongside the quarterly numbers, and reflects continued confidence in cash flow generation even as forward revenue guidance remains muted.

## FY27 Revenue Guidance

The company's formally disclosed FY27 revenue guidance is roughly 1.5–3.5% constant-currency growth. This is a relatively conservative band by historical standards, and has been read by analysts as a signal of cautious client spending and soft near-term demand, even though the company continues to describe a much larger long-term AI-driven opportunity.

## Structural Transition Risk Disclosure

Earnings-linked commentary and previews explicitly acknowledge that AI-driven productivity gains at clients can compress traditional IT services revenue over time, even as Infosys pursues a long-term AI services opportunity estimated at USD 300–400 billion by 2030. This is best treated as a disclosed structural transition risk: near-term services revenue faces AI-related pricing and volume pressure, while the company positions itself to capture a larger AI-native opportunity over the coming years.
