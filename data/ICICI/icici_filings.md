# ICICI Bank — Filings & Regulatory Disclosures

SOURCE: Curated from ICICI Bank's official performance review disclosure for the quarter ended March 31, 2026 (icici.bank newsroom).

DATE: 2026-03-31 (quarter-end); disclosed in Q4 FY26 performance review

## RBI-Directed Provisioning

Management disclosures for the quarter highlight additional standard asset provisions taken in line with RBI direction. These are described as being carried conservatively, meaning the bank appears to be provisioning ahead of any confirmed stress rather than reactively, which is generally read as a prudent, de-risking posture rather than a signal of deteriorating asset quality.

## Treasury Losses

The same disclosures acknowledge treasury losses for the quarter, again framed as being absorbed conservatively within the results. Combined with the standard asset provisioning, this suggests the bank is proactively taking hits on more volatile, market-linked components of its book while keeping core lending metrics such as gross and net NPA on an improving trend.

## Overall Filing Tone

Taken together, the quarter-end disclosures point to a bank managing known, disclosed risk items (regulatory provisioning, treasury marks) through conservative accounting choices, while core operating metrics such as loan growth, margin, and asset quality remain healthy. This distinguishes the risk profile as macro/rate-driven rather than credit-quality driven.
