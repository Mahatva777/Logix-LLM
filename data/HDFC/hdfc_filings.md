# HDFC Bank — Filings & Governance Disclosures

SOURCE: Curated from Sahi blog coverage of HDFC Bank's governance and stock commentary, and Reuters reporting on HDFC Bank's Q4 FY26 results.

DATE: 2026-06 (governance review disclosure); 2026-04-18 (Q4 FY26 results period)

## Merger-Related Margin Disclosure

Company and analyst commentary tied to the Q4 FY26 results repeatedly frames net interest margin, at roughly 3.38% on total assets, as structurally lower than the approximately 4% level the bank achieved before its merger. This is presented as an ongoing, disclosed effect of merger integration and changes in funding mix, rather than a one-off event, meaning margin normalization is likely to be a multi-quarter process rather than something resolved in a single reporting cycle.

## Governance Review Outcome

A June 2026 disclosure noted that an independent legal review examined allegations that had been raised by a former chairman of the bank, and found no evidence to support those allegations. This effectively closes out what had been an overhanging governance concern for the stock, and can reasonably be treated as a resolved risk item rather than an open one going forward.

## Market Positioning Despite Overhangs

Despite the margin compression and the now-resolved governance question, coverage continues to describe HDFC Bank as a consensus buy among analysts, even after a meaningful price drawdown in 2026. This suggests that structural franchise strength is viewed by the market as intact, with valuation derating treated as a sentiment and margin-cycle issue rather than a solvency or governance one.
